# Fenrua Public Content Guidelines

Status: Active public-interface guideline v0.1
Owner: Public Platform and Product & Domain
Last reviewed: 2026-07-14

Every substantive public page identifies its purpose, current state, interface,
input, output, trust boundary, failure mode, evidence, limitation, owner, and
next promotion gate where those fields apply.

- Use exact maturity and availability from the canonical register.
- State an unavailable or planned product plainly; do not substitute a mock
  result, screenshot, command, progress meter, or implied waitlist.
- Distinguish a specification, reference implementation, local fixture, public
  release record, signed observation, and hosted service.
- Keep exact claims adjacent to their evidence and limitations.
- Do not call an internal test, a static release manifest, or a public document
  an audit, certification, production service, uptime result, or guarantee.
- Keep chain observations subordinate to product and company identity unless a
  reader is on an observation-specific surface.
- Use direct technical language and bounded verbs: `states`, `records`,
  `checks`, `planned`, `reference`, `not available`, and `does not prove`.

Before the Local Trust Gate release, no public page may show its installation
command, SDK usage, API endpoint, product screenshot, result output, or an
availability statement.
