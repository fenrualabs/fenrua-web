# Fenrua — Current Direction and Launch Brief

**Owner:** Sam  
**Status date:** 14 July 2026  
**Document purpose:** Define the current product direction and the next launch sequence without turning long-term ambitions into automatic launch gates.

## Purpose

Fenrua is being developed as an AI-powered security, reliability, verification, evidence, and infrastructure service intended to make AI-assisted systems more dependable and useful.

The project is not currently being developed as:

- a speculative token project;
- P-FEN;
- FENswap;
- a trading or swap platform;
- a generic chatbot wrapper; or
- a marketing-only concept without operating infrastructure.

Do not restore those superseded directions unless Sam explicitly changes the product strategy.

## Existing foundation

Treat the following as established project inputs unless current evidence proves a specific technical regression:

- The business and legal registration path has been handled, and verification has been supplied.
- Dedicated real-world hardware and infrastructure are funded and maintained by Sam.
- The primary VPS and its mesh/encryption wiring are operational assets that must be preserved.
- Two live chain environments have accumulated approximately 600,000 combined blocks at the founder's latest stated milestone.
- Website, architecture, security, evidence, and infrastructure work already exist across the Fenrua repositories.
- Ongoing hosting, hardware, power, domains, and operating costs are real.
- Marketing, SEO, publisher relationships, and launch capability are available when the site and beta experience are ready.

These foundations should enable execution. They must not be repeatedly converted into requests for proof that has already been supplied.

## Near-term outcome

The near-term outcome is a controlled, technically honest public beta that:

- presents the current service clearly;
- exposes only capabilities that actually exist;
- preserves the primary infrastructure and security model;
- provides usable public and developer paths;
- is indexable and discoverable;
- has sufficient monitoring and recovery for the beta scope; and
- allows real users and partners to produce evidence for the next iteration.

This is not a claim of final global-enterprise maturity.

## Launch sequence

The current sequence is:

1. Complete the current website implementation and remove unfinished or contradictory user-facing paths.
2. Verify that public claims match implemented behaviour and evidence.
3. Complete technical indexing, metadata, sitemap, robots, structured-data, and performance readiness.
4. Prepare controlled-beta onboarding, operating visibility, support/feedback capture, and bounded safeguards.
5. Activate marketing and discovery when Sam selects the launch window.
6. Expand using real user evidence rather than speculative scope growth.

This sequence is directional. It is not permission to create a new permanent readiness register.

## Current-task rule

Sam's current prompt selects the active work.

Codex must not re-plan the entire launch whenever one deliverable is requested. Finish the selected deliverable, test it, and continue to the next directly dependent action.

## Block-height milestone

Sam may use one million blocks as a founder-selected timing, confidence, or public-story milestone. Do not silently reinterpret that number as a universal technical requirement or use it to block work that can be completed before the milestone.

## Protected invariants

The following remain protected unless a current task explicitly authorizes work on them:

- primary VPS mesh and encryption wiring;
- existing secrets, certificates, keys, peer identities, and trust relationships;
- live chain/node state;
- canonical production bindings;
- established business registrations and verification;
- active repositories and uncommitted work; and
- evidence required to reproduce or roll back infrastructure changes.

## Historical documents

Large engineering specifications, old launch mandates, security theses, audit reports, and previous upgrade tasks remain valuable references. Their unimplemented sections are not automatically part of the current beta scope.

Use a historical document only when the current task names it or selects one of its capabilities for implementation.
